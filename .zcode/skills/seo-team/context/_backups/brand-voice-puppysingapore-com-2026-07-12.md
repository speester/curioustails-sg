# Brand Voice: Puppy Singapore (puppysingapore.com)

This documents the voice as it currently exists on-site, based on live reads of the
homepage, contact page, and breed pages (corgi, golden retriever, british bulldog,
cavapoo). It is not a prescribed/aspirational voice, it is what's actually there today.

## Tone in three words
Warm, whimsical, informational (with a noticeable seam between the two halves, see
observation below).

## Who's reading and their anxiety
A Singapore-based buyer comparing specific breeds before a purchase decision, likely
browsing multiple breed pages back to back. Their anxieties, inferred from what the
copy tries to reassure:
- "Will this breed actually suit my home/lifestyle" (addressed via breed-characteristic
  filler sections on traits like trainability, grooming, exercise needs)
- "Is this a well-bred, healthy puppy" (addressed via "health certifications and
  ancestry records," "veterinary check-ups and vaccinations")
- "Is this going to be affordable" (addressed defensively rather than with a number:
  "Well-bred puppies are not cheap, but they will bring you joy instead of disgust")
- "Can I trust a seller I may never physically visit" (the site is delivery/shipping-first
  with no shop address advertised, so the copy leans on individual puppy bios with
  named personalities to build an emotional, pre-purchase bond before a WhatsApp chat)

> CONFIRM: this is an inference from copy patterns, not from the owner. Worth checking
> against real customer objections the owner hears on WhatsApp.

## Say / don't say

| Say | Don't say |
|---|---|
| Named, personality-led puppy bios ("Sora is a sweet Corgi girl with a warm brown-and-white coat that's simply made for cuddles") | Flat inventory language ("Corgi puppy, female, available now") |
| Breed traits framed as everyday practicalities (grooming, trainability, exercise needs) | Vet-textbook phrasing with no owner-relevance |
| Emoji as light accents around a puppy's name (e.g. "🐾 Akira 🐾") | Emoji stacked into every sentence or used on factual/breed-info paragraphs |
| Value framed as "well-bred puppies are not cheap, but..." (justify, don't apologize) | Discount/bargain language ("cheap," "lowest price," "sale") |
| "Health certifications and ancestry records," "veterinary check-ups and vaccinations" | Vague trust claims with no specifics ("100% healthy guaranteed") |
| WhatsApp-first calls to action ("message us for pricing, availability & viewing location") | Hard-close sales language ("buy now," "limited stock, act fast") |

## Sentence style
- Per-puppy bios open with a short, poetic, sensory line built around a physical
  feature or color, almost always a simile or metaphor: "give her the look of a tiny,
  perfectly painted woodland fox," "wears his red-and-white coat like a tailored
  little tuxedo, every marking crisp and storybook-perfect."
- Breed-info sections switch register entirely: short, plain, matter-of-fact sentences
  with little personality: "Corgis are known to be happy, intelligent and loving. They
  are easy to train due to their intelligence." "They are very easy to groom, just keep
  up with the brushing and shedding will never be a problem."
- Second person shows up mainly in the generic breed-info sections, addressing the
  reader's future ownership directly ("it is necessary that you...").
- Sentences are short to medium length throughout, no long-form storytelling beyond
  the single-line puppy bio.

## Observation (not a directive)
The two halves of the voice don't fully match: the per-puppy bios are genuinely
distinctive and warm, but the surrounding breed-info paragraphs read as generic,
slightly dated, AI-boilerplate-style filler (stiff phrasing like "it is necessary
that," thin claims with no specifics). If this team later touches these pages, that
seam is worth flagging to the owner as a content-quality gap, separate from this
being "the voice" to document as-is. This file documents the voice as it currently
exists; whether to evolve it is a separate decision for the team/owner.
