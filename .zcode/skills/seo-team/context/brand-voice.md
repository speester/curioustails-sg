# Brand Voice: William Goh Pet Farm (wgpetfarm.com)

This documents the voice as it currently exists on-site, based on live reads of the
homepage, About page, Contact page, puppies-for-sale page, and FAQ page. It is not a
prescribed/aspirational voice, it is what's actually there today. It should NOT be
assumed to match puppysingapore.com's documented voice (the two sites are structurally
similar in places, both being individual-puppy-listing pages, but they are separate
files and separate crawls; do not copy one onto the other).

## Tone in three words
Warm, sales-forward, listing-style.

## Who's reading and their anxiety
A Singapore-based buyer who is either already searching for this business by name
(the dominant traffic pattern per GSC: "william goh pet farm" is the top query at
position 2.0) or comparing specific breeds/listings before visiting in person. Their
anxieties, inferred from what the copy tries to reassure:
- "Is this puppy real, healthy, and available" (addressed via per-puppy detail fields:
  ID, gender, color, age, location, origin, HDB status, shown for every listing)
- "Will HDB let me keep this breed in my flat" (addressed with explicit HDB Approved
  checkmarks/crosses on every breed and every individual puppy listing)
- "Can I trust this seller enough to visit in person" (addressed by "Viewing Strictly
  By Appointment" plus a heavy, repeated push to see more photos/video on WhatsApp
  before committing to a visit)
- "Is this breed right for my home/lifestyle" (addressed via short personality-forward
  breed blurbs: trainability, energy level, apartment suitability)

> CONFIRM: this is an inference from copy patterns and the GSC query data, not from
> the owner. Worth checking against real customer questions the owner hears on
> WhatsApp or in person at the farm.

## Say / don't say

| Say | Don't say |
|---|---|
| Personality-forward breed blurbs ("incredibly intelligent," "adorable underbite," "great companions," "full of energy") | Flat, clinical breed-standard language with no personality |
| Explicit HDB Approved status (checkmark/cross) on every listing | Vague or absent HDB guidance, since flat-suitability is a real Singapore buyer concern |
| "View More Photos & Video on WhatsApp" as the dominant, repeated CTA | Burying the contact path or making buyers hunt for how to reach the farm |
| "Viewing Strictly By Appointment" (stated plainly, not apologetically) | Implying walk-ins are welcome, or hiding that a visit must be scheduled |
| Warm, aspirational framing ("delightful bundle of joy," "precious furry friend") paired with practical facts (age, color, origin) | Pure sentiment with no concrete listing details, or pure spec-sheet with no warmth |
| "Quality You Can Trust" as a trust anchor | Unverifiable superlatives beyond what's already claimed on-site (do not invent new proof points) |

## Sentence style
- Per-puppy listings are structured, not prose: short personality-forward opening line,
  then a compact field list (ID, gender, color, age, location, origin, HDB status).
- Breed-level copy runs short: single-clause personality descriptors strung together
  ("cute, loyal, funny"; "great for apartment living and hypoallergenic").
- Calls to action repeat verbatim and often ("View More Photos & Video on Whatsapp"
  appears throughout the puppies-for-sale listings), functioning almost as a structural
  refrain rather than a one-off CTA.
- FAQ/care content (vaccinations, feeding, toilet training) switches register to plain,
  instructional, second-person practical advice, separate from the sales-listing voice.

## Observation (not a directive)
Structurally this site reads a lot like a listings marketplace (per-puppy ID/gender/
color/age/origin/HDB-status fields, heavy repeated WhatsApp CTA) rather than leaning
into the "visit our farm" experiential angle the physical premises could support. The
warmth is there in the breed personality language, but the actual page architecture is
closer to inventory-first than experience-first. If the team leans this property into
its intended hyperlocal/"visit the farm" lane, that's a real content-strategy gap worth
flagging to the owner, separate from documenting the voice as it exists today.
