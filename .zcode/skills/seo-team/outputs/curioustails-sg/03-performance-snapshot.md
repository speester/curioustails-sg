# Performance Snapshot: Curious Tails (curioustails.sg)
**Report prepared:** 11 July 2026  
**Data period:** 11 April – 11 July 2026 (Q2/Q3 2026)  
**Analysis scope:** Singapore, English, organic search only  
**Client:** Curious Tails Pte. Ltd. | AVS-licensed pet shop, Balestier Road, Singapore

---

## Executive Summary

Curious Tails is an early-stage site (newly launched) with **minimal organic visibility** compared to three direct competitors. The site currently ranks for only 39 keywords, generating an estimated $25.63 in monthly organic traffic value—a **99%+ gap** behind competitors. Organic traffic is extremely low, averaging 1–3 sessions per day with no paid search presence. The data indicates the domain was recently created and lacks the content depth, keyword coverage, and authority needed to compete in this category.

**Immediate opportunities:**
- Expand keyword targeting (competitors rank for 300+ keywords each)
- Build out breed category pages (only 39 keywords vs 300–431 for competitors)
- Establish topical authority in "puppy for sale + breed + Singapore"
- Generate qualified backlinks to increase domain authority

## 3. Traffic Estimates & Estimated Traffic Value (ETV)

### What ETV Means
ETV is an estimate of organic traffic's monetary equivalent based on position, search volume, and CPC. A keyword at position 5 with 1,000 monthly searches worth $0.50 in ads would have an ETV of roughly $2.50/month for that keyword.

### Current ETV Performance
| Metric | Curious Tails | Wagatail.sg | Lovely Pets | Pup Life |
|--------|---------------|------------|------------|----------|
| **Total ETV (organic)** | **$25.63** | **$5,313.92** | **$8,144.43** | **$2,661.42** |
| **Estimated traffic cost (if paid ads)** | $18.29 | $3,592.47 | $4,886.77 | $1,721.38 |
| **Keywords ranked** | 39 | 431 | 411 | 307 |
| **ETV per keyword** | $0.66 | $12.32 | $19.81 | $8.67 |

**Key insight:** Curious Tails generates **99.5% less traffic value** than The Lovely Pets and **99.7% less** than Wagatail. The gap is driven by:
1. Severely limited keyword coverage (39 vs 300–431)
2. Poor ranking positions (mostly 31–100 vs competitor's 1–20 dominance)
3. Missing brand authority and backlinks (single digit referring domains)
