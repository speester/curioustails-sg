# How Much Does a Puppy Cost in Singapore? (2026 Price Guide)

Well-bred puppies in Singapore typically cost $2,500 to $9,000, with the exact price depending on breed, lineage, and breeder reputation.

Prices swing hard by breed — a Pomeranian can run under $2,000 while a well-bred Golden Retriever or Corgi routinely clears $8,000. Below is a breed-by-breed breakdown, what drives the price up, the cheapest legitimate route to owning a dog in Singapore, and what it costs to keep one after the purchase price is paid.

## How Much Does a Corgi Puppy Cost in Singapore?

A Corgi puppy in Singapore generally costs $3,000 to $9,000, with well-bred lines from established breeders often priced between $7,880 and $8,500.

Corgis are one of the most searched breeds on this site, and price varies widely by coat pattern, lineage, and whether the litter carries health screening papers. [The Lovely Pets lists Corgis in the $3,000–$9,000 range](https://www.thelovelypets.com/corgi-singapore.html), while [Income's cost-of-ownership guide](https://www.income.com.sg/blog/cost-of-dog-ownership-in-singapore) narrows well-bred Corgi pricing to $7,880–$8,500. Puppy Singapore's own [Corgi puppies](https://puppysingapore.com/corgi/) — including named litters like Sora and Pika — are viewable on the breed page, with exact pricing available on request via [Contact Us](https://puppysingapore.com/contact-us/).

One thing worth knowing before you fall for a Corgi photo: Corgis are **not** on HDB's approved small-breed list, so HDB flat dwellers should check the [official approved-breed list](https://www.nparks.gov.sg/-/media/avs/resources/dog-licensing/approved-small-breed.ashx) or this site's own [list of HDB-approved dogs](https://puppysingapore.com/list-of-hdb-approved-dogs-in-singapore/) before committing.

## How Much Does a Golden Retriever Puppy Cost in Singapore?

Golden Retriever puppies in Singapore typically cost $8,000 to $9,000, among the higher end of common breeds sold here.

[The Lovely Pets](https://www.thelovelypets.com/golden-retriever-singapore.html) and [Income's cost guide](https://www.income.com.sg/blog/cost-of-dog-ownership-in-singapore) both put Golden Retrievers at the top of the market — Income specifically cites $8,000–$9,000, the highest figure of any breed in its comparison. Like Corgis, Golden Retrievers are not on HDB's approved list, so they need a condo or landed home. Puppy Singapore's [Golden Retriever puppies](https://puppysingapore.com/golden-retriever/) page has current litters; pricing is shared directly by the team on request.

## How Much Does a Toy Poodle or Cavapoo Cost in Singapore?

A Toy Poodle typically costs $6,000 to $8,000 in Singapore, while a Cavapoo usually runs $2,500 to $3,500 — one of the more affordable popular crossbreeds.

[Income's guide](https://www.income.com.sg/blog/cost-of-dog-ownership-in-singapore) puts Toy Poodles at $6,000–$8,000. For Cavapoos, [Singapore Puppies' cost breakdown](https://singaporepuppies.com/breeds/cavapoo/cost/) states an initial cost of $2,500–$3,500, plus roughly $1,500 a year in food, grooming, and vet care once the puppy is home. Both breeds are popular precisely because they suit smaller Singapore homes — see Puppy Singapore's own [Cavapoo puppies](https://puppysingapore.com/cavapoo/) page for current litters like Zacian.

## Why Are Well-Bred Puppies in Singapore So Expensive?

Well-bred puppies cost more in Singapore because of health screening, ancestry documentation, and a limited supply of ethical breeders on a small island.

Every reputable breeder absorbs real costs before a puppy is ever listed: vet check-ups, vaccinations, and the paperwork behind "health certifications and ancestry records" — the same claims Puppy Singapore makes for its own litters, alongside its AVS Licence (No. AF23009, shown in the site footer) and, where applicable, limited registration with the puppy's country of origin and the Kennel Club of Singapore. Land is scarce and breeding space is expensive in Singapore, which pushes costs onto every litter regardless of breed. As the site's own copy puts it: well-bred puppies are not cheap, but they're bred to be worth it, not bred to be a bargain.

## What's the Cheapest Way to Get a Dog in Singapore?

Adoption is the cheapest legitimate route to dog ownership in Singapore, with fees typically running $70 to $600 depending on the shelter.

[Income's cost guide](https://www.income.com.sg/blog/cost-of-dog-ownership-in-singapore) cites SPCA adoption fees of $70–$350, with other shelters charging $300–$600. HDB residents who want a larger dog have a specific legal path too: [Project ADORE](https://puppysingapore.com/project-adore/) lets HDB flat owners adopt a local mixed-breed dog up to 55cm tall, subject to microchipping, sterilisation, licensing, and a mandatory obedience course. Puppy Singapore runs its own active [dog adoption programme](https://puppysingapore.com/dog-adoption-singapore/) alongside its puppy sales — worth a look before committing to a purchase, especially for first-time owners on a budget.

## What Ongoing Costs Come After the Puppy Price?

Beyond the purchase price, dog owners in Singapore should budget roughly $1,000 to $1,500 a year for food, grooming, vet care, and licensing.

The official [AVS dog licence fee](https://avs.nparks.gov.sg/pets/licensing-a-pet/information-on-dog-and-cat-licences/licence-fees/) is $15 a year (or a one-time $35 lifetime licence) for a sterilised dog, versus $90 a year for a non-sterilised dog — a 10% rebate applies if you pay via GIRO, online, or AXS. On top of that, [Income's cost guide](https://www.income.com.sg/blog/cost-of-dog-ownership-in-singapore) estimates roughly $292/year in routine vet care, $366/year in food, and $274/year in grooming for a small-to-medium dog, putting a 12-year lifetime total near $10,937 excluding the purchase price. Puppy Singapore's own [puppy obedience training programme](https://puppysingapore.com/puppy-obedience-training/) — covering car, potty, stairs, noise, crate, obedience, and leash training — is a further cost worth budgeting for in the first few months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a Corgi HDB-approved in Singapore?**
No. Corgis are not on HDB's official approved small-breed list, so they can only be kept in condos, landed homes, or other private housing.

**Can I keep a Golden Retriever in an HDB flat?**
No. Golden Retrievers exceed HDB's size and breed restrictions and are not on the approved list — they require a condo or landed property.

**Is a Cavapoo good for HDB or apartment living?**
Yes, generally. Cavapoos are small, low-shedding, and adapt well to apartment life, though individual temperament and exercise needs still vary by litter.

> NEEDS FROM YOU / CONFIRM: this Cavapoo/HDB answer is inferred from general breed-size guidance, not a confirmed line-item on HDB's approved list. A competitor (Pretty Pets Kennel) currently holds the featured snippet for this exact question per `04-geo-audit.md` — worth the client double-checking against the official HDB list before this line goes live, since getting it wrong on a housing-legality question is worse than not answering at all.

**What is the cheapest pet to own in Singapore?**
Adoption is the cheapest route to pet ownership in Singapore, with shelter fees as low as $70 and no breeder premium attached.

**Is an F1 or F2 Cavapoo better?**
Neither is objectively "better" — F1 Cavapoos (first-generation Cavalier x Poodle) tend to have more predictable coat and temperament traits, while F2 and later generations vary more but may shed less depending on the individual dog.

**How much does it cost to license a puppy in Singapore?**
Licensing costs $15 a year for a sterilised dog or $90 a year if unsterilised, with a one-time lifetime option available for sterilised dogs at $35.
