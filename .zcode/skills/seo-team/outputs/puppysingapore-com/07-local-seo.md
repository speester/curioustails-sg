# Local SEO: Puppy Singapore (2026-07-12)

## Why this section looks different

A normal Local SEO Manager run leans on a Google Business Profile (GBP) for three of its four sections: review management, GBP posts, and profile-completeness fixes. **puppysingapore.com has no GBP, and that is a deliberate strategic choice, not a gap.**

Per `context/client.md`: the owner has assigned hyperlocal / local-pack positioning to his other two properties — **Curious Tails** and **wgpetfarm.com** — both of which run active, GBP-backed local SEO. puppysingapore.com's lane is different: topical-authority, buyer's-guide content aimed at organic/informational search, not the local 3-pack. A DataForSEO Google Maps search for "Puppy Singapore" confirms no listing exists under this name; the address/phone that public web search sometimes attributes to "Puppy Singapore" actually belongs to wgpetfarm.com's real GBP (4.5 stars, 64 reviews) — a mix-up between the two related sites, not evidence of a missing profile that should be fixed.

So, for this engagement:

- **Section 1 (Reviews)** — not applicable. No GBP means no review stream to monitor or respond to. The site's `/reviews/` page is a customer-photo carousel with no written reviews to action.
- **Section 2 (GBP Posts)** — not applicable. No profile to post to.
- **Section 3 (Profile completeness / Windsor.ai insights)** — not applicable. No profile to score, no GBP insights connector to pull from.

None of the above are flagged as `> NEEDS DATA:` — they aren't missing data, they're the correct output of a domain that has intentionally opted out of local-pack SEO. What follows (citations, NAP consistency, and a forward-looking note) is the work that's actually relevant here.

**Findings:**
1. **No name variants found** on the pages checked — "Puppy Singapore" is used consistently, no stray "Puppyland Sg" or "William Goh Pet Farm" text surfaced on-page (those only appear in unconfirmed third-party listings per client.md, not on-site).
2. **Phone digits are correct everywhere** (880-66180 / 8806 6180 — same 8 digits, no typos) — but the **display formatting is inconsistent**: the contact page shows "+65 88066180" (no internal space) instead of the canonical "+65 8806 6180" (space after the first 4 digits). This is a minor but fixable polish item before citations go out, since citation directories will copy whatever format they scrape or whatever is submitted.
3. **The footer carries no visible NAP text at all** — only the AVS license number and legal links. That's consistent with the no-address strategy, but means the footer currently gives directories nothing to scrape; any citation building should use the canonical block below as the source rather than expecting to lift it from the footer.

**Recommendation:** update the contact page phone display to "+65 8806 6180" (matching canonical spacing) before or alongside citation submissions, so every directory that copies from the site gets the same format the agency will also be hand-entering elsewhere.

## If the no-GBP decision changes

Purely informational — not a recommendation to act now, since the current no-GBP strategy is intentional and owned by the client.

If the owner ever revisits this for puppysingapore.com specifically, a GBP would unlock: eligibility for the local 3-pack / Maps results on "puppy shop Singapore"-type queries, a dedicated review stream (the site currently has no written reviews at all, only a photo carousel), GBP Posts as a lightweight content-distribution channel, and Windsor.ai-based profile/insights reporting the way Curious Tails and wgpetfarm.com already get. It would also require deciding on a public address or a service-area-only profile, and would put puppysingapore.com in more direct local competition with the owner's own wgpetfarm.com GBP — worth weighing before any future decision, not something to solve today.
