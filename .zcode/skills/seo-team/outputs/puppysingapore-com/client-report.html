Executive Summary

The verdict, plainly

70–80%
Organic traffic decline from peak, over the last 16 months (Google Search Console, our own first-party data)

This is real and sustained, but it is not a penalty — there is no manual action on file, and the decline lines up with specific, fixable page-level problems rather than a site-wide algorithmic hit. The single biggest lever is unglamorous: a broken sitemap file, a template bug that has been serving the word "Adaptability" as the meta description on 30+ money pages, and a handful of structural heading defects are very likely suppressing pages that were once ranking well. Underneath that, the opportunity is real — dog-adoption-singapore already ranks around position 5 for two keywords worth 4,400 searches a month each, and the domain is already earning 22 citations inside Google AI Overviews, ahead of one of its three named competitors. At today's rankings the site is worth an estimated $2,109/month in organic traffic value, against $5,300–$8,100/month for the two strongest competitors on a near-identical keyword footprint — the gap is entirely in position quality, not coverage, which means it is closeable with the fixes in this report rather than a rebuild.

Findings

What we found

Five specialists reviewed the site independently — technical crawl, keyword position, analytics, AI search visibility, and local/citations. Here is what each one found, in plain English, and what we'll do about it.

1

Technical Health

The sitemap that lists every money page is broken

Example: page-sitemap.xml — almost certainly the file listing the homepage and all 30+ breed pages — times out every time it's requested (confirmed twice, independently, as a server error).
What we'll do: track down why this specific sitemap query is timing out (most likely a caching or plugin conflict), fix it, and resubmit to Google Search Console. With no Google Business Profile as a fallback, clean crawl discovery is the whole game for this domain.

Every breed page's meta description reads "Adaptability"

Example: the search-result snippet for Corgi, Golden Retriever, Dachshund, and at least 10 other breed pages is a stray 12-character label — "Adaptability" — leaked out of the breed-traits table by a template bug.
What we'll do: one template fix resolves it across all 30+ pages at once; real, benefit-led descriptions are already written and ready to paste in (see 05-onpage-fixes.md).

A page full of puppies literally headed "Zamazenta" and "Vulpix"

Example: the all-available-puppies page uses individual puppy names — including several Pokémon names — as H2 headings, mixed in with the real breed-category headings Google actually needs to read the page correctly.
What we'll do: restructure so puppy names become card titles, not heading tags, and keep H2s reserved for the real breed categories.
2

Keywords & Content Opportunity

Dog adoption is the single best keyword opportunity on the domain

Example: "dog adoption singapore" and "dog for adoption singapore" — 4,400 searches a month each — already rank around position 5.
What we'll do: fix the page's technical bugs and rewrite it answer-first to push it into the top 3 — this is optimization of a page that already works, not new content.

The buying-guide layer is wide open, and a competitor is already winning it

Example: prettypetskennel.com runs breed "Traits, Care & Cost" guides, a poodle-mixes hub, and a dog-license guide — content types that don't exist yet on puppysingapore.com, on topics the site is otherwise well positioned to own.
What we'll do: build breed-cost companion posts and topical hubs against the priority list in 01-keyword-map.md, starting with the breeds that already have traffic.
3

Performance & Analytics

The decline is real, sustained, and not a penalty

Example: Search Console shows clicks and impressions falling steadily since roughly December 2024, steepening further in January 2026 — with no manual action on file. The sharpest position collapses are on /all-available-puppies/ (position 6.2 → 33.9) and /dog-adoption-singapore/ (7.1 → 54.3).
What we'll do: treat this as a page-level technical and content problem, and fix the exact pages that collapsed first.

The gap versus competitors is position quality, not coverage

Example: the client ranks for roughly the same number of keywords as its two strongest competitors (~410–430 each) — but they have 3.5–5x more top-3 rankings, which is why their traffic value is 2.5–4x higher.
What we'll do: focus effort on the ~60 keywords already sitting at position 4–20 ("striking distance") rather than chasing brand-new keyword territory.
4

AI Search & GEO (AI Overviews)

The domain is already being cited by AI Overviews — this isn't starting from zero

Example: 22 confirmed AI Overview citations for puppysingapore.com, ahead of named competitor Pretty Pets Kennel (13), though trailing The Lovely Pets and Wag A Tail (50+ each).
What we'll do: extend the format that's already working — the breed pages carrying citations today — rather than reinventing it.

No price is published anywhere on the site — every competitor's citation leads with one

Example: both /corgi/ and /cavapoo/ gate pricing entirely behind a WhatsApp message, while The Lovely Pets, Pretty Pets Kennel, and Wag A Tail all state a concrete dollar figure directly in their own AI Overview snippets.
What we'll do: pending your decision below, publish at least an indicative price range — the single highest-leverage fix identified anywhere in this audit for AI-citation growth.
5

Local Presence

No Google Business Profile is correct here — this is strategy, not a gap

Example: hyperlocal, GBP-backed positioning is deliberately reserved for Curious Tails and wgpetfarm.com. puppysingapore.com's lane is topical-authority content, and a DataForSEO Maps search confirms no listing exists under this name.
What we'll do: nothing on GBP for this domain — it would put you in local competition with your own wgpetfarm.com listing. Effort goes into citations and NAP consistency instead.

Name and phone number are consistent — display formatting isn't

Example: the phone number on the Contact page reads "+65 88066180" (no internal space) instead of the canonical "+65 8806 6180" — same digits, inconsistent grouping.
What we'll do: standardize the display format before submitting the 14 identified directory citations, so every listing matches.
The Plan

30 / 60 / 90-day roadmap

Sequenced from every specialist's own fix-order list — technical first (it's cheap and unblocks everything else), then content and citations, then authority and measurement.

Days 1–30

Fix & stabilize

Critical technical fixes + metadata
  1. Fix the broken sitemap (504 timeout) and resubmit in Search Console
  2. Fix the "Adaptability" meta-description bug — one template fix, 30+ pages
  3. Fix the dual-H1 bug across all 10 adoption pages — one template fix
  4. Restructure heading tags on the all-available-puppies page (remove the Pokémon-named H2s)
  5. 301-redirect the orphaned duplicate Cocker Spaniel page
  6. Roll out the rewritten titles/descriptions for the homepage and top 5 breed pages
  7. Add FAQPage schema to the adoption pages (the FAQ content already exists)
  8. Standardize the phone number display format on the Contact page
Days 31–60

Content & citations

Publish the highest-ROI content, start building presence
  1. Two decisions from you unblock this phase — the pricing disclosure and the business-name/service-area disclosure (see Small Print below)
  2. Finish and publish the "How Much Does a Puppy Cost in Singapore" guide (60–70% drafted already)
  3. Answer-first rewrite of /dog-adoption-singapore/ to push it from position ~5 toward the top 3
  4. Publish breed-cost companion posts for Corgi and Golden Retriever — riding pages that already carry AI citations
  5. Submit the 4 highest-value directory citations (SGPBusiness, YellowPages SG, Pet Directory Singapore, dogs.sg)
Days 61–90

Authority & measurement

Build the topical hub, round out citations, track recovery
  1. Launch the "Dog Breeds in Singapore" hub page — the internal-link spine for all 40+ breed pages
  2. Publish the "Types of Poodles / Poodle Mixes" guide
  3. Fill the highest-value missing breed pages (Yorkshire Terrier, Samoyed, Goldendoodle, Bernese Mountain Dog)
  4. Add Product/Offer schema to breed pages, once the pricing decision is made
  5. Submit the remaining medium- and low-effort directory citations
  6. Re-measure all 5 baseline metrics below and check how many striking-distance keywords converted to top 10
