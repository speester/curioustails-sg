## Why this order

**Piece 1 (visit-experience) goes first and is this wave's draft.** Per `04-geo-audit.md`, wgpetfarm.com already holds local-pack rank #2 for "pet farm singapore" and organic rank 3 for "sungei tengah dog farm," with **zero direct competitor** contesting that specific lane — the animal-visit SERP is dominated by non-breeder content (Hay Dairies, Sundowner Nature Experience Centre). This is the one topic on the whole map where the client isn't fighting thelovelypets.com or Pretty Pets Kennel for the citation. It's also the only page in this plan that doesn't need a resolved price or years-in-business number to be genuinely useful — visit logistics (appointment process, what happens on-site, how to get there) can be written in full today, which matters because every other topic on this list is price-gated or years-gated to some degree.

**Pieces 2–4 (Corgi, Cavapoo, Dachshund) are the direct citation-gap recovery play**, per the brief and per `04-geo-audit.md`'s headline stat: wgpetfarm.com holds **zero** AI Overview citations for its three named specialty breeds, while thelovelypets.com is cited on 47+ breed-price queries including those same two breeds. Cavapoo is flagged as the cleanest opening (both named competitors rank pos 24–68 there) so it's sequenced right after Corgi, which already has an existing, indexed, page-1-ranking page to build on (`01-keyword-map.md` confirms `/corgi-puppies-for-sale/` already ranks pos 2 for "corgi price singapore"). Dachshund is sequenced third because its page wasn't independently crawled in the technical audit — there's more URL/schema uncertainty to resolve before writing.

**Pieces 5–6 are P2 support content.** "How much does a puppy cost" is a large, unowned, high-intent PAA query per the GEO audit's fan-out map, but it can only be answered honestly in ranges/drivers-of-price terms until the client unblocks real pricing — so it's sequenced after the breed pages, not before. The AVS trust piece is the lowest-volume item on this list but the cheapest citation win available: it needs no pricing and no years-in-business number to be complete, just the AVS License AF23009 (already confirmed, already citable) explained in plain language, with a link out to the AVS breeder registry as the credibility anchor.

## Publishing cadence

Realistic for a one-operator content pipeline reviewing each piece before publish: **one piece every 1–2 weeks**, in the order above, starting once piece 1's `> NEEDS FROM YOU` markers are answered. That's roughly 6 pieces across 6–10 weeks. Do not batch-publish pieces 2–4 back to back without a review gap — the years-in-business and pricing conflicts documented in `04-geo-audit.md` touch all three breed pages, and getting the resolved language wrong once means fixing it in three places instead of one. Fix the conflict in piece 1 or via direct owner answer first, then reuse the exact same resolved wording across 2, 3, and 4.
