Executive Summary

The verdict, plainly

2.0 vs 39.8
Average position on "william goh pet farm" (branded) vs. average position across the full tracked query set (Search Console, 16 months)

That gap is the clearest way to read this domain's traffic pattern: people who already know the farm's name find it almost instantly, but the site is nearly invisible to everyone searching by breed, category, or intent instead of by name. That's a fixable, page-by-page problem, not a brand problem — the local pack backs this up directly: wgpetfarm.com holds an uncontested #2 local-pack slot for "pet farm singapore" (210 searches/mo), proving the hyperlocal strategy works when Google has the signals to work with, yet it's completely absent (0 of 3) from the packs for "dog breeder singapore" (260/mo) and "puppy for sale singapore" (1,000/mo), the two highest-intent searches in its category.

Second point worth flagging up front: technical health here scores 46/100, meaningfully weaker than sister property puppysingapore.com's 58/100. The two biggest drivers are a complete absence of LocalBusiness schema anywhere on the site and severe server response times (4.1–8.5 seconds time-to-first-byte on every page tested) — both of which land hardest on exactly the domain built to win local search. This is the priority fix: the farm's real, visitable premises and 4.5-star reputation are genuine assets the website currently does almost nothing to reinforce to Google.

Findings

What we found

Five specialists reviewed the site independently — technical crawl, keyword position, analytics, AI search visibility, and local/citations. Here is what each one found, in plain English, and what we'll do about it.

1

Technical Health

No LocalBusiness schema anywhere — on the one domain built to win local search

Example: every page's structured data emits only a generic Organization node (name + URL). No LocalBusiness, PetStore, address, phone, hours, or rating markup exists anywhere. The full NAP (name, address, phone) itself appears as plain, unmarked text on exactly one page — Contact — and nowhere else, not even the footer.
What we'll do: add a PetStore/LocalBusiness node carrying the confirmed GBP details (address, phone, hours, 4.5★/64 reviews) to the homepage, and add NAP + hours to the sitewide footer. This is the single highest-leverage fix on the whole audit.

Every page tested loads slowly — 4.1 to 8.5 seconds before the page even starts rendering

Example: server response time (Lighthouse): Home 4.1s, /puppies-for-sale/ 5.4s, /cavapoo-puppies-for-sale/ 8.5s. Google's own guidance is under ~0.6s. This is a hosting/caching problem, not a content one.
What we'll do: a hosting and caching review — this likely blocks the crawl efficiency and Core Web Vitals gains needed for every other fix in this report to land fully.

Duplicate and empty heading tags on two of the site's most important pages

Example: the homepage splits its H1 into two separate tags ("William Goh" then "Pet farm"); /puppies-for-sale/ has its real H1 immediately followed by a second, completely empty <h1></h1> left over from a responsive-design artifact.
What we'll do: combine into one clean, keyworded H1 per page — quick template fixes, already drafted in 05-onpage-fixes.md.

Two literal typos sit in live meta descriptions

Example: the homepage's description reads "Over 40 years of breeding exprience" (missing the "e," and at 38 characters far too short to be useful); the FAQ page's description is missing its leading "W" — "...e've got answers to your puppy questions here."
What we'll do: rewrite both to full length with the typos fixed — ready to paste in today, no dependencies.
2

Keywords & Local-Pack Position

"Pet farm singapore" is a genuine, uncontested win — and the model for what's possible

Example: wgpetfarm.com holds local-pack rank #2 of 3 for "pet farm singapore" (210 searches/mo), right behind a nature-experience centre and ahead of a well-known goat farm — no direct pet-breeder competitor contests this lane at all. The site also ranks page 1 organically for "sungei tengah dog farm" and "pets farm singapore."
What we'll do: protect and expand this cluster — it's proof the hyperlocal strategy works once the signals are in place, and it's the template for the fixes below.

Absent from the two highest-intent local packs in the category

Example: "dog breeder singapore" (260/mo) — Pretty Pets Kennel holds #1, wgpetfarm.com is 0/3. "puppy for sale singapore" (1,000/mo) — The Lovely Pets holds #1, wgpetfarm.com is 0/3 there too.
What we'll do: once the LocalBusiness schema and footer NAP are live, review GBP category weighting and posting/photo cadence against these two competitors specifically — the highest-intent searches on the whole map are currently fully ceded.

The domain's biggest-volume terms are ranking far off page 1

Example: "vet near me" / "vets near me" (9,900 searches/mo each — by far the largest volume the domain touches anywhere) sit at position 69–98 on /24-hour-vet-clinics/. The domain's own address term, "sungei tengah" (720/mo), ranks only position 44, buried on Contact.
What we'll do: refresh the vet-clinics page (it carries a stale 2021 date) as a realistic referral-traffic play, and give "sungei tengah" a proper, richly-detailed home on the rewritten Contact page rather than a thin utility listing.
3

Performance & Analytics

The branded-vs-non-branded split explains the whole traffic pattern

Example: of 996 tracked keywords, only 3 (0.3%) are branded — and all 3 rank position 1–3. The other 993 (99.7%) are category/breed terms, and the large majority sit off page 1. Estimated traffic value has fallen 56% in six months ($4,250/mo in February to $1,880/mo in July) even as the number of ranking keywords rose slightly — existing rankings are sliding down, not disappearing outright.
What we'll do: prioritize the 207 keywords already sitting at position 4–20 ("striking distance") — they need a push, not a rebuild, to reach page 1.

The real competitor gap is The Lovely Pets, not Pretty Pets Kennel

Example: wgpetfarm.com actually leads Pretty Pets Kennel on both breadth and quality (495 vs. 432 keywords, 11.1% vs. 9.7% page-1). But The Lovely Pets, with fewer total keywords (411), wins 44.3% of them on page 1 — four times wgpetfarm.com's rate — and earns 4.3x the estimated traffic value ($8,144/mo vs. $1,880/mo).
What we'll do: treat this as a ranking-quality problem, not a content-coverage problem — the breed pages already exist, they need to be strengthened, not replaced.
4

AI Search & GEO (AI Overviews)

Zero AI Overview citations for the three breeds the homepage names as specialties

Example: the homepage names Corgi, Cavapoo, and Dachshund as specialty breeds, but wgpetfarm.com currently holds zero AI Overview citations for any of them. Competitor thelovelypets.com is cited across 47+ tracked breed-price queries, including for those same two breeds (Corgi, Dachshund) — roughly 3.5x wgpetfarm.com's citation rate for anything.
What we'll do: rewrite the Corgi, Cavapoo, and Dachshund pages with dense, self-contained answer paragraphs near the top (breed + HDB status + what's included + how to buy) — the shape AI Overviews lift most reliably — plus Product/FAQPage schema.

The "years in business" conflict is actively degrading trust-signal extraction

Example: the site itself states four different figures for the same claim — homepage "over 40 years," breed-page meta descriptions "50 years," breed-page body copy "since the 1960s," and the Google Business Profile "AVS-licensed Singapore breeder since 2020." An AI system trying to answer "how long has this breeder operated" has no consistent number to pull from the site's own pages.
What we'll do: hold every new page to the confirmed, uncontested facts (AVS license AF23009, 4.5★/64 reviews) until the owner resolves which years figure is correct — see Small Print below. This is an open item, not a completed fix.
5

Local Presence

A similarly-named listing with a very different rating — worth the owner's awareness

Example: Google's "people also search" for wgpetfarm.com surfaces "William Goh Kennel" (3.2★/45 reviews, 23 Seletar West Farmway 1 — a different address entirely, in Sengkang/Seletar, not Sungei Tengah).
What we'll do: flag this to the owner as a conversation, not an SEO fix — it isn't correctable through wgpetfarm's own GBP listing. Worth clarifying whether it's a legacy/family-linked listing and, if so, whether to request Google differentiate or update it.

8 reviews at 3-star-or-below have unknown reply status

Example: of 64 reviews, 55 are five-star (86%) — a genuinely strong base — but 8 sit at 3-star-or-below (5×1★, 2×2★, 1×3★) and reply status can't currently be checked; the profile isn't connected in Windsor.ai.
What we'll do: once Windsor.ai is connected (or dashboard access is granted), pull the actual review text and draft on-brand replies, prioritizing these 8 first.

The GBP's listed website link uses http, not https

Example: DataForSEO's live pull shows the GBP website field set to http://wgpetfarm.com/, while the site itself correctly redirects to https.
What we'll do: a one-line fix in the GBP dashboard — update the listed URL to match the site's actual protocol.
The Plan

30 / 60 / 90-day roadmap

Sequenced from every specialist's own fix-order list — technical and schema first (cheapest, unlocks local-pack eligibility), then citation-recovery content, then citations and relationship items that depend on the owner or on Windsor access.

Days 1–30

Fix & stabilize

Schema, structure, and the hosting problem
  1. Add LocalBusiness/PetStore schema with the confirmed NAP, hours, and 4.5★/64-review rating — the top-priority fix
  2. Add NAP + opening hours to the sitewide footer, not just Contact
  3. Fix the duplicate/empty H1 bugs on Home and /puppies-for-sale/
  4. Fix the two meta-description typos (Home, FAQ)
  5. Add FAQPage schema to /faq/ — content already exists, near-zero cost
  6. Open a server response time investigation with the host — 4.1–8.5s TTFB blocks everything downstream
  7. Embed a Google Map + visible opening hours on /contact-us/
  8. Remove the stray FlyWP staging URL leaking into the sitewide Person schema
Days 31–60

Citation recovery

Breed pages, the visit-experience piece, GBP posts
  1. Publish "Visiting William Goh Pet Farm: What to Expect" — ~60–70% drafted, pending the owner's answers on visit logistics
  2. Rewrite the Corgi page with an answer-capsule intro + FAQPage schema — it already ranks well organically, it just isn't being cited
  3. Rewrite the Cavapoo page — the cleanest open gap; both named competitors rank weakly here (position 24–68)
  4. Rewrite the Dachshund page using the same pattern once confirmed live
  5. Roll out the 4 drafted GBP posts (pet-farm win, visit experience, breed specialties, Cavapoo spotlight)
  6. Add "Dog trainer" as a GBP category; increase photo volume specifically for Corgi, Cavapoo, and Dachshund litters
Days 61–90

Citations, reviews & relationships

Execute the citations plan, catch up on reviews, flag brand confusion
  1. Submit the priority citations — AVS/NParks public registry first, then Facebook, Instagram, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, Yellow Pages SG
  2. Once Windsor.ai connects, pull real review text and reply to all 8 sub-4-star reviews first
  3. Have the William Goh Kennel brand-confusion conversation with the owner
  4. Re-measure the 5 baseline metrics below and check how many striking-distance keywords converted to page 1
