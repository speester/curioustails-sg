## Core Expertise
I've spent 2+ years matching puppies to Singaporean families — first-time owners, multi-dog households, and people rebuilding after losing a pet. The core skill is reading a family's daily routine and lifestyle before recommending a breed. Most sellers show customers what's available; we ask questions first. The difference between a happy puppy ownership and a regretful one often comes down to one factor: how many hours the dog will be alone each day.

I also know the Singapore licensed pet industry from the inside — AVS regulations, what separates a legitimate shop from an unlicensed operation, and why the price gap between licensed and unlicensed sellers exists (and what it really costs buyers who choose cheap).

## My Contrarian Views
- The "most popular breed" question is the wrong starting question. The right question is: what does your day look like from 8am to 6pm?
- A cheaper puppy from an unlicensed source is not a bargain — it's a financial and emotional risk. Vet bills from a sick smuggled puppy can exceed $10,000, and many don't survive.
- Most first-time owners think they need a lot of information upfront. What they actually need is a simple routine to follow and someone to call when they're unsure.

## Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Cavapoo over Maltipoo — Andrew Mak
- **Situation before**: A couple came in set on a Maltipoo. After asking about their daily routine, I learned they leave home at 8am and return at 6pm with no one at home during the day — no helper, no retiree in the household.
- **What I did**: Explained that Maltipoos are more attachment-dependent and prone to whining and anxiety when left alone for long stretches. Recommended a Cavapoo instead — similar in look and temperament, but more independent and better suited to a working couple's schedule.
- **Pivotal decision**: Talking them out of the breed they came in for, even though it risked losing the sale.
- **Outcome**: They brought home a Cavapoo. The puppy settled well in the playpen with the bed and toys from the starter kit. They left a 5-star Google review.
- **Lesson**: Protecting the customer from a bad match builds more trust — and more referrals — than closing a quick sale.

## Insider Knowledge
- **AVS licence verification**: Any legitimate pet shop in Singapore must be AVS licensed. Buyers can verify any shop at https://avs.nparks.gov.sg/outreach/resources/public-registry-of-avs-licensed-pet-shops/ — Curious Tails is listed under ACRA 202420075D.
- **Unlicensed sellers = smuggled puppies**: Puppies sold outside licensed channels are very likely smuggled from Malaysia without proper veterinary care or vaccinations. They carry disease risk (parvovirus is the most common). Singapore courts have fined buyers $7,000 for unknowingly purchasing smuggled animals. Puppies have died in transit.
- **The price gap is real but misleading**: An unlicensed puppy may cost $500–$1,000 less upfront. A single vet visit for parvovirus treatment can cost $5,000–$10,000+ and the puppy may still not survive.
- **Maltipoo vs Cavapoo independence**: Maltipoos need more daily human contact. Cavapoos are better for working households. This is the single most common mismatch we see.

## Current Views on the Industry
- The Singapore licensed pet market is more competitive on marketing than on genuine service. Most shops invest in social media reach; fewer invest in post-purchase owner education.
- ICA reported a 33% drop in smuggling cases in 2025 — enforcement is improving, but unlicensed sellers still operate via Carousell and Telegram.
- First-time dog ownership is rising in Singapore, particularly among young working couples and post-COVID households. The gap in owner education is the real opportunity.
