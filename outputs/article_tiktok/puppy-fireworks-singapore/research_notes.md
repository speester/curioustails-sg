# Research notes — puppy-fireworks-singapore

Source article: https://curioustails.sg/blog/puppy-fireworks-singapore
Author: Nelson & Kim, Curious Tails (AVS-licensed pet shop, Balestier, Singapore)
Core claim: A dog scared of fireworks in Singapore can be managed with early preparation; the plan is a covered safe room, a 3-4 week desensitisation countdown, calm handling on the night, and smart walk timing.

Most concrete detail (anchors the hook): Singapore's big fireworks are predictable — National Day on 9 August, plus the NDP rehearsal Saturdays through July and early August, and the New Year countdown at Marina Bay. The rehearsals mean the ninth is usually NOT the puppy's first exposure, so prep should start weeks before.

Key facts pulled from the article:
- Puppies hear a wider, sharper range of sound; HDB high-rise acoustics amplify the bangs.
- Safe room = quietest interior room + the starter-kit playpen, curtains drawn, windows shut, aircon on to muffle sound.
- Desensitisation: 3-4 weeks out, firework sounds low volume during meals/play, raise volume only while relaxed, pair with treats.
- On the night: feed and toilet before dark, background noise, stay calm, check collar tag + microchip in case of a bolt.
- Do NOT punish fear, do NOT force exposure; comforting a scared puppy does not "reward" fear.
- Escalate to a vet for severe noise phobia; never self-medicate.

No fabricated statistics used. All video claims trace to the article.
